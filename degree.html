My Degree!

As I am not even mature yet, my degree background is not as broad as I want it to be. However, I have through a lot furing this short period of life.

Here you can see my "stages" of growing up, in a way.

This was the first 'classroom' I attended. Though we were just kids, the lessons learned at kindergarten went beyond ABCs and 123s. It was here I discovered the joy of exploration, the importance of sharing, and how the simplest of activities could teach lifelong values. It was a place where every day felt like a new adventure, and the playground was as much a classroom as the inside. These early years laid the foundation for my love of learning, and I'll always remember them fondly.

This was the first real step into the world. In elementary school, I transitioned from the simplicity of kindergarten to more focused lessons in reading, math, and science. It was a time filled with excitement for new knowledge, friendships that deepened, and the beginnings of responsibility. From the first time I wrote my name to the moments of solving my first math problems, these early school years played a crucial role in building my confidence and curiosity for the future.

High school was a turning point in my educational journey. It was a time of discovering my interests, challenging myself with more advanced subjects, and building important skills for life beyond the classroom. Friendships grew deeper, and I became more independent, managing studies, extracurricular activities, and personal goals. High school wasn’t just about academics—it was about growing as a person, finding my passions, and preparing for the opportunities that awaited after graduation.
